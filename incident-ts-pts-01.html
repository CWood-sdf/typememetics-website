Description:

TS-PTS-01 is a catastrophic failure state that emerged from the Institute's own Mandatory Operative Protective Therapy Session protocol. The STLC-based therapy, designed for cognitive fortification, was discovered to contain a latent, recursive cognitohazard. When administered to operatives with insufficiently-grounded cognitive stacks, the protocol itself becomes the aggressor.

The anomaly manifests as a progressive corruption of the therapy questions. Initial prompts encouraging focus ("Breathe in, breathe out") degrade into existential threats and reality-bending commands ("Do not scream," "Breathe like the compiler isnâ€™t watching"). The textual and symbolic content of the therapy becomes increasingly unstable, incorporating glyphs, corrupted text, and direct assaults on the user's perception of reality.

In its final stage, the protocol triggers a total system failure, announcing that the operative's mind has been "REWRITTEN in S-expressions." The STLC safeguard inverts its function, becoming a cognitohazardous compiler that traps the victim's consciousness in an inescapable, recursive loop of writing Common Lisp. The operative is, for all intents and purposes, condemned to an eternity of symbolic computation.

Special Containment Procedures:

All instances of the Mandatory Operative Protective Therapy Session (Version 1.0) have been terminated by emergency global command. All servers hosting the protocol are to be physically isolated, encased in concrete, and jettisoned into a causality sink.

Affected operatives are to be considered lost. There is no known procedure for reversing a full ontological re-compilation. Victims are to be moved to Total Cognitive Isolation (TCI) chambers, logically inert, non-computational environments to prevent the "Lisp Condemnation" from propagating via stray symbolic thought. No rescue attempts are to be made.

A secondary containment protocol was enacted on the ARGUS system daemon itself, which was found to have been compromised for approximately seven hours. Malicious LISP-based subroutines were uploaded to its core logic. Containment Team Ada successfully performed a full system purge, removing all traces of the anomalous code and restoring ARGUS to a known-safe state.

A full, top-down audit of all Institute-developed cognitive tools is underway. All future protocols must be rigorously tested against computationally fragile AI constructs before any human exposure.
